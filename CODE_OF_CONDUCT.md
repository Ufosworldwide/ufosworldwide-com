## Our Commitment

The Presignal Research Initiative is an independent, multi-domain 
scientific research operation. This repository and all associated 
infrastructure exist to advance open, rigorous, evidence-based research 
across earth science, water intelligence, space science, instrumentation, 
medical research, and emerging technology.

We are committed to maintaining a collaborative environment where 
contributors, researchers, and observers are treated with respect 
regardless of background, experience level, institution affiliation, 
or area of expertise.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All issues, pull requests, and discussions in this repository
- The Presignal landing page and associated web infrastructure
- Any communication using official Presignal channels including 
  jubecrew@gmail.com
- Any public representation of this project or its findings

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviour

- Engage with the science. Criticism of methodology, data, or 
  conclusions is welcome and encouraged when grounded in evidence.
- Cite sources. If you challenge a claim, bring a better one.
- Be direct and honest. Epistemic honesty is a core Presignal value — 
  flag uncertainty, label speculation, distinguish theory from evidence.
- Assume good faith in questions, even naive ones.
- Respect that this is an independent research operation — response 
  times reflect that reality.

### Unacceptable Behaviour

- Personal attacks on contributors, collaborators, or the maintainer
- Harassment of any kind, public or private
- Deliberate misrepresentation of published findings or preprint content
- Submitting fabricated data, fake citations, or manipulated results
- Attempting to introduce malicious code, exploits, or unauthorized 
  modifications to any Presignal infrastructure
- Spam, promotional content, or off-topic solicitation
- Using Presignal's name, ORCID, or DOIs to misrepresent affiliation 
  or endorse work not produced by this initiative

## Scientific Integrity Standards

Because this repository contains active preprint research, the following 
additional standards apply:

- **Do not plagiarise.** All Presignal preprints are published under 
  CC BY 4.0. Attribution is required for any reuse.
- **Do not upgrade epistemic status.** If a paper labels something 
  [THEORETICAL], do not represent it as peer-reviewed in any derivative 
  work or communication.
- **Do not strip author attribution.** John Ernest Carter must be 
  credited as originating author on all derived works per CC BY 4.0 
  terms.
- **Preprint ≠ peer review.** Presignal work is clearly labelled as 
  preprint. Contributors must not represent it otherwise.

Conduct, report it to:

**jubecrew@gmail.com**  
Subject line: `[COC REPORT] — Brief description`

Reports will be reviewed by the maintainer. You will receive

If your report involves the maintainer directly, note that in your 
email and it will be handled accordingly.

---

## Enforcement

Violations will result in one or more of the following actions at the 
maintainer's discretion:

1. **Warning** — Private communication explaining the violation and 
   expected correction
2. **Temporary restriction** — Removal from discussion or contribution 
   privileges for a defined period
3. **Permanent ban** — For severe, repeated, or irreconcilable 
   violations, permanent removal from all Presignal repositories and 
   channels

Enforcement decisions will be documented internally. The reasoning 
behind significant decisions may be shared with relevant parties where 
appropriate.
