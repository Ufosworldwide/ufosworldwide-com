01 // What You Can Make With These

Chameleon mica is not glitter — it's a finely milled reflective pigment that shifts colour with the angle of light. It suspends cleanly in clear bases, blends without streaking, and adds a dimensional depth that flat pigments can't replicate. Here's what people actually use it for:

✨ Resin Art & Epoxy Pours
The colour shift is at its most dramatic in clear resin. Pour a thin layer over black resin and the mica blooms into a living, shifting surface. Used in everything from jewellery to river tables.
💅 Nail Art & Gel Polish
Dust over tacky gel before curing for a mirror-like chameleon finish. Works over both dark and light bases — black base gives the most dramatic shift.
🧼 Handmade Soaps & Bath Bombs
Cosmetic-grade safe. Adds an iridescent pearl shimmer to melt-and-pour or cold-process soaps. Holds colour through the saponification process.
🥤 Custom Tumblers & Cups
Mix into epoxy base coat for wrapped tumblers. The colour shift catches light from every angle as the tumbler rotates — the effect looks entirely different throughout the day.
💄 Cosmetics & Body Art
Blend into loose powder, eyeshadow base, or highlighter formula. Photograph beautifully under studio lighting — the multi-angle shift reads as dimensional glow on camera.
🎨 Canvases & Mixed Media
Mix into acrylic medium or pour paint for abstract work. The surface of the finished piece changes as the viewer moves — a built-in optical experience no flat paint can replicate.
🕯️ Candles & Wax Melts
Stir into melted wax before pouring for shimmering container candles. The mica settles into a metallic shimmer layer as the wax cools — catches candlelight beautifully.
🪵 Wood Resin Inlays
Fill routed channels or voids in turned wood with tinted resin carrying chameleon mica. The inlay shifts colour under different lighting conditions — no two viewing moments look the same.
🐟 Fishing Lures & Tackle
UV-stable and water-resistant when sealed in epoxy. The multi-angle flash profile mimics the iridescent scales of baitfish — a proven attractor pattern in both freshwater and saltwater.
