SafeKit
A Presignal Public Health Concept — Niagara Region
Concept Framework — v1.0

One kit.
Four emergencies.
Zero hesitation.

SafeKit is a community harm reduction concept designed to address the four most common preventable emergency scenarios: fentanyl-contaminated drug use, opioid overdose, accidental poisoning, and diabetic crisis — consolidated into a single, accessible, clearly labelled kit.

⬤ Presignal Initiative — Public Health Domain
⚠
Fentanyl Contamination: The Hidden Risk

Fentanyl and its analogues (carfentanil, nitazenes) are now detected in stimulants, pressed pills, and street MDMA — not just opioids. A user who doesn't identify as an opioid user may have no Narcan nearby and no fentanyl test strips. SafeKit addresses this gap by treating fentanyl screening as a universal pre-use step, not an opioid-specific one. Julie's insight: if Fenty is in the package, the whole calculus changes.

MODULE 01
Pre-Use
🧪
Fentanyl Risk Screen
Anti-Risk — Test Before Use

The first line of defence. Before anything enters the body, test it. Fentanyl test strips detect fentanyl and many analogues in seconds using a small dissolved sample. A positive result triggers the rest of the kit protocol.

Kit Contents
  • BTNX fentanyl immunoassay test strips (6–10 pack)
  • Small sample cup or spoon (water dissolution)
  • Instruction card — result reading guide
  • QR code → local overdose prevention line
  • Foil pouch (waterproof, pocket-size)
MODULE 02
OD Response
💉
Opioid Overdose Response
Naloxone — Narcan / Kloxxado

If fentanyl is present or opioid OD is suspected: act immediately. Naloxone reverses opioid receptor binding within 2–5 minutes. Nasal spray preferred for layperson use. High-dose formulation (8mg) recommended given fentanyl potency.

Kit Contents
  • Naloxone nasal spray — 8mg/0.1mL (2 doses)
  • Nitrile gloves (2 pair)
  • Recovery position instruction card
  • CPR face shield
  • "Call 911 first" laminated card with Good Samaritan Act note
MODULE 03
Broad Spectrum
☠️
Poison & Toxin Response
Non-Narcotic — Household & Environmental

Expanded beyond drug use: cleaning agents, pesticides, carbon monoxide, plant toxins, medication misuse. Not a treatment kit — a stabilization-and-call kit. Includes activated charcoal (where appropriate) and Poison Control direct line.

Kit Contents
  • Poison Control Canada card: 1-800-268-9017
  • Activated charcoal (EZChar) — adult dose, single use
  • Eye flush saline (30mL squeeze bottle)
  • Skin decontamination wipes (chemical exposure)
  • Symptom checklist — ingestion vs. inhalation vs. contact
  • Do NOT induce vomiting instruction card
MODULE 04
Metabolic
🩸
Diabetic Crisis Relief
Hypoglycaemia — Conscious & Unconscious

Hypoglycaemia can mimic intoxication — and is often misidentified in harm reduction settings. Rapid glucose restoration for a conscious person; glucagon for unconscious. Julie's nursing lens: this module alone could save lives in shelters and social services settings.

Kit Contents
  • Dex4 glucose gel (3 tubes, 15g each — conscious use)
  • Baqsimi glucagon nasal powder 3mg — unconscious use
  • Glucose tablets (fast-dissolve, 10-count)
  • MedicAlert identification card prompt
  • Hypoglycaemia vs. intoxication differentiation card
  • 15/15 rule instruction card (15g glucose, check 15 min)
Principle One
Screen before use. Test strips are not judgment — they are intelligence.
Principle Two
Broaden the net. Harm reduction is not only for opioid users.
Principle Three
One kit. Clear labels. No hesitation in an emergency.
