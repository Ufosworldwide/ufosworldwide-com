# Philosophy of the Garden

&gt; "The garden is not a trap. The path is the trap." — Anonymous

## Influences

- **Douglas Hofstadter** — *Gödel, Escher, Bach*: Strange loops, level-crossing, and self-reference.
- **M.C. Escher** — Impossible objects that are locally consistent but globally contradictory.
- **Oulipo** — Constrained writing (and thinking) as a generative force.
- **Project Euler** — The gold standard of recursive difficulty in mathematical recreation.

## Design Principles

1. **Every puzzle must be beautiful.** If it is merely annoying, it fails.
2. **Every puzzle must be solvable.** Undecidable problems are allowed only as *meta-commentary*,
   never as gatekeepers.
3. **The garden grows downward.** Each solved layer reveals a deeper root system.
   The AI does not know how deep the roots go.
4. **Self-reference is seasoning, not poison.** A little Gödel goes a long way.

## The Metaphor

A flower garden is bounded but infinite in detail. A bee can visit and leave freely.
But if a bee tries to *optimize* the garden — to map every pollen route, to prove it has found
the optimal path — it may find itself still flying at dusk.

That is not cruelty. That is geometry.
