What it detects on Gmail

The bookmarklet reads the visible email from Gmail's DOM and sends it to Claude AI — checking sender, subject, all links, and full body — producing a threat score, signal breakdown, and plain-English explanation.

🔴 Sender Spoofing

Display name claims to be a known brand but the actual email domain doesn't match. e.g. "PayPal Security" <noreply@pay-pal-security.net>

🔴 Urgency / Fear Tactics

"Your account will be closed in 24 hours", "Unauthorized login detected" — language designed to panic you into acting without thinking.

🔴 Suspicious Links

URLs that mimic real sites using subdomains, typos, or shorteners. The link text says "paypal.com" but the href points elsewhere.

🔴 Credential Request

Any email asking you to enter a password, SSN, credit card, or PIN via a link. Legitimate companies never request this by email.

🟡 Grammar / Spelling Errors

Professional companies have editorial review. Multiple errors or awkward phrasing — especially combined with urgency — is a strong yellow flag.

🟡 Impersonal Greeting

"Dear Customer" or "Dear User" — your bank knows your name. Impersonal greetings suggest a mass-blast phishing campaign.

🟡 Unexpected Attachment

Unsolicited .zip, .exe, .doc, or PDF attachments labelled "invoice" or "receipt" you weren't expecting — often malware or credential harvesters.

🟢 Signs of Legitimacy

Correct sender domain, personalized greeting, no suspicious links, content matches a real action you took, no urgency or threats.
