Seasonal Source Water Temperature as a Predictive Indicator for Legionella Outbreak Risk
First correlation analysis connecting Ontario source water temperature to confirmed Legionella case counts. Demonstrates a 2–6 week predictive window before outbreak probability rises — a signal that has been in the public data for 60 years.

Water Science · 2026
A Multi-Parameter Source Water Risk Framework for Legionella Prediction in Ontario Cooling Tower Systems
Integrates 60 years of PWQMN data, five years of Ontario AWQI records (18,114 incidents), and PHO Legionella surveillance 2019–2025. Grand River scores 14/15 on composite risk index. Low chlorine incidents parallel Legionella cases across all five years. Provides scientific basis for a provincial cooling tower registry.

Water Science · 2026
The Disinfection Gap: Trihalomethane Exceedances as Sentinel Indicators of Legionella Vulnerability in Ontario Agricultural Watersheds
Documents the first province-wide organic-chlorine demand crisis in Ontario's five-year AWQI record. THM exceedances sustained across all 12 months of 2024–25. Low chlorine AWQIs peak 4–8 weeks before Legionella summer outbreak season. Grand River health units carry 2.6× the AWQI burden of Canadian Shield health units.
